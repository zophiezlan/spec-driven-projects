Proposal: Development of the AOD Care Charter Implementation Guide
Submitted by: NSW Users and AIDS Association (NUAA)
Prepared for: Centre for Alcohol and Other Drugs (CAOD)
Date: 14 May 2025
Proposed Budget: $21,200
Overview
This proposal outlines NUAA's plan to develop a succinct, practical Implementation
Guide to accompany the Alcohol and Other Drug (AOD) Care Charter. The aim of the
Guide is to improve uptake and integration of the Charters principles by AOD service
providers, especially in settings where stigma and discrimination reduction strategies
are underdeveloped. The resource will be modelled on successful tools such as the
ACON LGBTQ+ Inclusive Guidelines and will incorporate consumer voice, practical
tools, and policy-ready examples.
Background and Rationale
The AOD Care Charter was developed through a collaborative co-design process
involving clinicians and consumers and has been well received. However, feedback
from the co-design working group and broader consultations highlights the need for a
practical guide that translates the Charters principles into actionable steps.
This need is particularly pressing in sectors and services that lack mature frameworks
to address stigma and discrimination. By oering real-world examples, policy
templates, and training resources, the Guide will help services embed the Charter's
principles into daily practice and organisational culture.
Objectives
1. Develop a user-friendly Implementation Guide aligned with the four themes of
the AOD Care Charter.
2. Provide clear, actionable examples for each theme that services can implement.
3. Curate and link to relevant training materials, policy templates, and external
resources.
4. Ensure strong representation of consumer voice through targeted consultation.
5. Deliver a resource that supports cultural and clinical safety, workforce capability,
and stigma reduction.
Proposed Methodology
1. Project Initiation and Scoping
Review Charter themes, co-design outputs, and existing implementation guides
(e.g. ACON guidelines)
Conrm scope and deliverables with funding agency
Develop a concise project plan with timelines and outputs
2. Consultation and Co-Creation
Establish a time-limited Implementation Guide Steering Committee including
CAOD, NUAA, NADA, LHD and consumer representatives.
Conduct consultations with clinicians and consumers including the Stigma and
Discrimination Working Group, the CAODs Consumer Reference Committee
and Quality in Treatment Committee.
Ensure remuneration for all consumer contributors in line with NUAAs
reimbursement policy
3. Content Development
Structure content around the four key Charter themes
Include for each theme:
o A brief explanatory summary
o 23 practice examples (e.g. inclusive intake forms, sta scripts, signage)
o Links to relevant training (e.g. stigma reduction, trauma-informed care)
o Recommended policies and templates
4. Peer Review and Finalisation
Submit draft guide for review by working group, consumers, and clinicians
Incorporate feedback and produce a polished, ready-to-distribute version
Optional: prepare companion slides or short explainer for training settings
Deliverables
1 x succinct AOD Care Charter Implementation Guide (print-ready PDF and
accessible digital format)
1 x summary slide deck for use in training and presentations
1 x brief report on consumer engagement process
